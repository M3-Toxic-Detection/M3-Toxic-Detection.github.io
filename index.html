Seeing the Unseen, Hearing the Unspoken: Detecting Cloaked Chinese Toxicity with Multi-Modal Cognitive Agents

Multi-Modal Multi-Agent Framework (M³-Agent)

Anonymous submission
Paper Code

Abstract

Despite the notable capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs) in detecting harmful content, they remain vulnerable to cloaked Chinese toxicity attacks that disguise malicious Chinese content via visual glyph dismantling or phonetic variation. Such vulnerability arises from tokenization limitations that fail to capture the multi-modal interplay of Chinese semantics, phonetics, and glyphs. We propose M³-Agent—a training-free framework that simulates human cognitive perception by projecting Chinese text into visual and acoustic modalities. Specifically, our framework employs decoupled Visual, Audio, and Text agents to perceive the shape, sound, and meaning of Chinese inputs respectively, integrating these signals through a complementary fusion strategy. Extensive experiments demonstrate that M³-Agent significantly outperforms existing methods and commercial APIs. Notably, M³-Agent empowers lightweight open-source models to surpass large closed-source LLMs, validating the critical role of multi-modal perception in robust Chinese toxicity detection.

Framework Overview

Figure 2: Overview of M³-Agent framework. The input text is projected into three modalities and processed by specialized agents, with results aggregated via Logical-OR fusion.

Three Cognitive Agents

Visual Agent
"The Eye"

Perceives glyph-based attacks through rendered images. Detects character dismantling, radical substitution, and emoji injection by treating text as visual patterns.

Text-to-Image VLM 448×448 RGB
Audio Agent
"The Ear"

Detects phonetic attacks via synthesized speech. Captures homophones, near-sound substitutions, and tonal deviations by bypassing misleading glyphs.

Text-to-Speech Audio-LLM 24kHz
Text Agent
"The Brain"

Serves as the semantic anchor for high-level reasoning. Handles explicit toxic content that doesn't rely on visual or phonetic cloaking.

Complementary Fusion: Logical-OR Strategy

ŷ = dvis ∨ daud ∨ dtxt

Unlike majority voting, our Logical-OR strategy ensures that toxicity detected in any modality is captured. Cloaked attacks are typically perceptible in only one modality while appearing benign in others—majority voting would silence correct signals through a "blind majority" effect.
